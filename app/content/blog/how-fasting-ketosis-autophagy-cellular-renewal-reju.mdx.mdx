### Autophagy and Cellular Housekeeping

Autophagy is a natural cellular maintenance process associated with fasting and nutrient availability.

The word autophagy comes from Greek and is commonly translated as “self-eating.” Although the term may sound unusual, the process plays an important role in how cells break down and recycle damaged or unnecessary components.

During autophagy, cells identify and recycle damaged proteins and other cellular material. A simple way to understand it is to think of autophagy as the body's internal housekeeping system at the cellular level.

Interest in autophagy grew significantly after Yoshinori Ohsumi received the 2016 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries related to the mechanisms of autophagy.
